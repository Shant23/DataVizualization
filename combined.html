Insights gleaned from Health, Military and Educational spending accross the G20

Examining outcomes in terms of health and wellbeing may be interesting when contrasted with spending accross healthcare, military and educational spending.

The few graphics below will examine some health and wellbeing outcomes, namely, life expectance, mortality rate and happiness scores in the different countries accross the G20.


Starting with the mortality rate for male, female as well as infant mortality, and contrasting that to healthcare spending.



The mortality rate seems to decrease as healthcare spending increases. The only exception is The United States where there is a higher than expected mortality rate despite a very high per capita helathcare spending.


Life expectancy is illustrated next and contrasted to bith military and healthcare spending.


Life expectancy over the past decade has been steadily increasing in the developing countries in the G20, thus it is not suprising to see that almost every country has a similar life expecancy despite their healthcare and military spending.


As a a conclusion let's look at the happiness scores accross the G20 as calculated by the world happiness report group. It appears that happiness is most correlated with educational and healthcare spending.
